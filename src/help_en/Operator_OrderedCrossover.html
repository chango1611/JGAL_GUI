Ordered Crossover

The ordered crossover consists of choosing two random points p1 and p2, which indicates the segment for crossover between the two chromosomes, as shown below:

B= (b1b2...b(p1-1)bp1...b(p2-1)bp2...bm) and
C= (c1c2...c(p1-1)cp1...c(p2-1)cp2...cm)

Where bi are the genes of B and ci the genes of C. These chromosomes are replaced by new chromosomes of the form:

B'= (b1b2...b(p1-1)bp2...bmcp1...c(p2-1)) and
C'= (c1c2...c(p1-1)cp2...cmbp1...b(p2-1))
